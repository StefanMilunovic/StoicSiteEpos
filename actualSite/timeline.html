470 BC - 280 BC

MAJOR STOIC INFLUENCES

Socrates - Influenced every school after him - the Stoics through his students Antisthenes and Plato.
Crates - Cynic school, Diogenes. Asceticism, virtue in agreement with nature.
Stilpo - Megarian school/Dialecticians. Logic, strict monism, self control.

334 BC - 129 BC

THE EARLY STOA

ZENO of Citium


Often known as a founder of Stoicism.
He created a school based on idea that it is virtuous to maintin a will in accord with nature.

106-43 BC

THE MIDDLE STOA

CICERO

"Glory follows virtue as if it were it's shadow."
Technically not a stoic, but were influenced by Stoic thought.
His commentaries are one of the major surviving sources of information about early and middle Stoa

4 BC - 101 AD

SENECA & RUFUS - LATE STOA

Seneca


Shaped the understanding of Stoicism for later generations

Rufus


Advocated for philosophical education of women and children.
Tutor of Epictetus.

55-135 AD

EPICTETUS

One of the most important stoics.
Born as a slave, he saw philosophy as a way of life.
We should accept out fate and remember that we only control our thoughts, actions and desires.

121 - 180 AD

MARCUS AURELIUS

Often viewed as a philosophy king.
Devout stoic and last of The Five Good Emperors of Rome.
Mostly influenced by Epictetus he wrote Meditations that serves as a philosophical legacy.
He explores duty, moderation and love of fate.
Restored 4 Chairs of Philosphy in Athens.
